Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 1, 2026

1. Introduction

SnapAPI (“we”, “us”, or “our”) provides screenshot and PDF generation APIs. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our website and services. By using SnapAPI, you agree to this policy.

2. What we do not do with your content

We do not store customer page content for our own purposes. URLs you submit are loaded only to perform the requested render in real time. We do not use your screenshots or PDFs for advertising, profiling, model training, or persistent analytics. We do not operate a data warehouse of the visual or textual content of pages you capture.

3. Cloudflare R2 and generated files

Output files (images and PDFs) are written to Cloudflare R2 object storage solely so we can return a download or public URL in the API response. This storage is a technical delivery mechanism, not a product feature for browsing or analyzing your assets. Retention, access controls, and lifecycle for those objects follow your configuration of the underlying bucket and applicable Cloudflare terms. We encourage you to treat returned URLs as you would any temporary delivery link unless you configure longer retention.

4. Account and operational data

To operate the service (authentication, API keys, billing tiers, quotas, and abuse prevention), we may process limited account and technical data such as email addresses, API identifiers, usage counts, request metadata (e.g. timestamps, endpoints, target URLs as needed for logs), and server logs. This processing is for service delivery, security, and compliance, not for building advertising profiles from the content of pages you render.

5. Cookies and sessions

Our dashboard and login flows may use cookies or similar technologies (e.g. session cookies) to maintain your session. These are used for authentication and security, not for cross-site tracking or behavioral advertising.

6. Processors and subprocessors

We rely on infrastructure providers (including hosting, databases, and object storage such as Cloudflare R2) to run the service. Their processing is governed by their respective terms and, where applicable, data processing agreements.

7. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, or alteration. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

8. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain personal data, or to object to processing. Contact us at the address below to make a request. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

9. International transfers
